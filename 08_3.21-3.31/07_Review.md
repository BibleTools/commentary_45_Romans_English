# Review

We offer the following questions to aid in studying, reviewing, or facilitating group discussions.

1. In verse 21, Paul writes that the righteousness of God has been "made known apart from the law." What is the significance of this statement in the context of the Jewish legal system of Paul's time?
2. In verse 22, Paul states that this righteousness is "through faith in Jesus Christ." How does this differ from the Jewish understanding of righteousness through obedience to the law?
3. According to verse 23, all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God. What does this mean in relation to the idea of righteousness being attained through obedience to the law?
4. Verse 24 says that we are "justified by his grace as a gift, through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus." What does this mean and how does it relate to the previous verse?
5. In verse 25, Paul writes that Jesus was "put forward as a propitiation by his blood." What does this mean and how does it relate to the concept of righteousness?
6. According to verse 27, where is boasting excluded? What is the significance of this statement?
7. In verse 28, Paul states that we are justified by faith apart from works of the law. How does this compare to the Jewish understanding of justification through obedience to the law?
8. How does the idea of being justified by faith impact our relationship with God and our understanding of salvation?
9. In verses 30-31, Paul concludes by saying that there is one God who will justify the circumcised by faith and the uncircumcised through faith. What is the significance of this statement in light of the Jewish/Gentile divide in Paul's time?
